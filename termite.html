Termite Control: Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Schedule regular professional termite inspections (at least annually)
  • Keep wood and cellulose materials away from foundation
  • Fix leaky pipes and drainage issues promptly
  • Maintain proper ventilation in crawl spaces and attics
  • Seal cracks and gaps in your foundation
  • Keep gutters clean and ensure proper drainage away from foundation
  • Store firewood at least 20 feet away from your home
  • Remove dead tree stumps and roots near your property
  • Maintain at least 6 inches between soil and wooden parts of your home
  • Consider pre-treatment during construction or renovation

Don'ts

  • Don't ignore signs of termite activity (mud tubes, hollow wood, discarded wings)
  • Don't use untreated wood for construction near soil
  • Don't allow water to accumulate near your foundation
  • Don't stack firewood against your house
  • Don't use DIY termite treatments without proper knowledge
  • Don't disturb active termite colonies (can cause them to spread)
  • Don't ignore wooden structures during inspections
  • Don't create wood-to-soil contact around your property
  • Don't plant shrubs too close to foundation walls
  • Don't wait for visible damage before taking action
